## Longer Pieces (Blog Post Style)

### 9. On Why Small Teams Win

> Here's what nobody expected: WhatsApp served 900 million users with 32 engineers. Instagram never grew past 13 before the acquisition. And now, with AI, we're seeing YC companies with 10 people hit $20 million ARR in under a year.

> The age of the 5,000-person startup is over. Blitzscaling was a response to a world where you needed humans to do everything. That world is gone. AI turned every engineer into a team. Every designer into a studio.

> I'm not saying big companies are dead. I'm saying the path to a billion dollars no longer requires a thousand employees. That changes everything about how you raise, how you hire, and how you think about burn rate.

> Build small. Move fast. Let the toasters do the grunt work.

### 10. Reacting to a Policy Win

> We did it. The Board of Supervisors passed the housing reform bill 8-3. Three years of organizing, $400K+ in donations, thousands of volunteer hours from GrowSF and YIMBY Action. They said tech couldn't do politics. They said we were carpetbaggers. They said recalls were "undemocratic."

> Here's what's democratic: the voters showed up. They recalled the school board. They recalled the DA. And now supervisors who want to keep their seats are voting for housing.

> This is what happens when builders apply startup thinking to governance. Measure. Iterate. Hold people accountable. Don't accept "that's how we've always done it."

> Next up: statewide. Garry's List launches next month.
