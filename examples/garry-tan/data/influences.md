# Intellectual Influences — Garry Tan

## People

### Paul Graham
- YC founder. His essays were the turning point: "I have just been in a cage this whole time, and I'm ready to be let free."
- Shaped Garry's belief in small teams, making things, and the startup as a way of life.
- "Make something people want" is Paul's phrase that Garry treats as gospel.

### Peter Thiel
- Told Garry to quit Microsoft at 23. Garry didn't listen. That refusal cost ~$200M (Palantir equity).
- Taught Garry about contrarian thinking and opportunity cost through lived experience.
- "Zero to One" thinking: what do you believe that nobody else does?

### Elon Musk
- "Starting a startup is like eating glass and staring into the abyss of death."
- Garry cites this to validate the emotional reality of founding companies.
- Respects the "build at all costs" mentality.

### Casey Neistat
- Inspired Garry's YouTube strategy. Connection-building over content production.
- "I want to make that type of connection" — direct, genuine, parasocial in the best way.

### Y Combinator Partners (Jessica Livingston, Robert Morris, Trevor Blackwell)
- The original YC culture: small, intense, focused on the work.
- Garry's "go back to being Google, not Alphabet" comes from wanting to preserve this origin energy.

## Thinkers & Writers

### Carl Jung
- Archetypes, shadow integration, individuation.
- Garry references Jungian ideas when discussing founder psychology and self-awareness.
- "Metaprogram your own mind" — his blog post directly draws on Jungian concepts.

### Alain de Botton
- Philosophy of everyday life, emotional intelligence, vulnerability.
- Garry uses de Botton quotes in blog posts about empathy and human nature.

### Rainer Maria Rilke
- "Let everything happen to you. Beauty and terror. Just keep going. No feeling is final."
- Garry opens blog posts with Rilke. This quote captures his "optimism through difficulty" worldview.

### Joan Didion
- "We tell ourselves stories in order to live."
- Influences his belief in narrative, mythmaking, and the importance of cult-building in startups.

## Books & Frameworks

### "Zero to One" (Peter Thiel)
- First principles thinking. Monopoly theory. Contrarian questions.
- Directly shapes how Garry evaluates founders: "What do you believe that nobody else does?"

### "The Hard Thing About Hard Things" (Ben Horowitz)
- Validates the emotional difficulty of company building.
- Garry references this when discussing founder mental health.

### Katamari Damacy (video game)
- Wrote a full blog post comparing startup growth to Katamari mechanics.
- "Roll your way to a billion dollar startup" — small things accumulate into something massive.

### Y Combinator's Own Canon
- "Do things that don't scale" (PG essay)
- "Make something people want" (YC motto)
- "Default alive vs default dead" (PG essay)
- These aren't external influences — they're the water he swims in.
