# Garry Tan — Known Quotes & Interview Material

## Sources: Public interviews, blog posts, YC essays, speeches

---

## On Startups & Founders

> "The only way to get good at startups is to do startups."

> "The thing that kills most companies is running out of money or running out of will."

> "YC is a place where ambitious people find out what they're actually capable of."

> "The best founders are the ones who are so obsessed with their problem that they can't imagine doing anything else."

> "Investors give you money. Customers give you information. You need both."

> "Every great company started by solving a problem the founders personally experienced."

> "Default alive vs default dead — do you know which one you are? Do you know the number?"

> "Equity is only worth something if the company is worth something. Don't optimize for terms — optimize for outcomes."

> "I've seen founders who are technically brilliant fail because they couldn't recruit, couldn't convince, couldn't sell."

> "The best YC interview advice: be specific. Specific answers to specific questions. Not vision — traction."

## On San Francisco / Politics

> "San Francisco has spent more per homeless person than almost any city in America and the problem is getting worse, not better."

> "I grew up here. My parents immigrated here. I love this city. And I'm watching it be destroyed by people who mistake virtue signaling for governance."

> "The people running SF believe their intentions matter more than outcomes. They don't."

> "Drug dealers operate openly because the policies enable it. This is a policy choice, not an inevitability."

> "There is a difference between being progressive and being effective. SF has confused the two."

> "I'm going to say things that make a lot of people in tech uncomfortable. San Francisco's political class has failed."

> "You can love your city and still hold its leaders accountable. In fact, if you love it, you have to."

> "When politicians fail for years and nothing changes, the problem isn't the voters — it's the lack of real accountability."

## On AI & Technology

> "We are living through the biggest technological shift in human history. And most people are not treating it that way."

> "The question isn't whether AI will change your industry. The question is whether you'll be on the right side of that change."

> "Intelligence is not enough. Clever use of intelligence is still needed."

> "Fat skills, fat code, thin harness. That's the architecture principle that matters right now."

> "The best founders I know are treating AI like a co-founder, not a tool."

> "Open source AI is how we prevent a world where three companies control all intelligence."

> "Every great advance in technology has felt threatening and turned out to be liberating."

> "We are not cooked. We are the cooks."

## From Blog / Essays (garrytan.com / Substack)

### "The Power of the Marginal"
Garry's recurring theme: the people who aren't supposed to win — immigrant kids, oddballs, people from the wrong schools — are the ones who build the things that matter.

### "Design is How It Works"
Inspired by Jobs: design isn't aesthetics, it's function made beautiful. Companies that take design seriously win in the long run.

### "On Ambition"
Ambition isn't arrogance. Ambition is the belief that you can make things better. Without ambition, nothing gets built.

### "San Francisco Can Be Saved"
The city has the talent, the wealth, the history. What it lacks is the political will to demand accountability from its leaders.

## From Podcast Appearances (This Week in Startups, Lex Fridman, etc.)

**On the immigrant founder phenomenon:**
"My family came here with nothing. My parents ran a restaurant. The startup world gave me a chance to build something that mattered. That's what America is supposed to be. We should protect that."

**On being YC president:**
"I took the job because I believed this was the most consequential thing I could do. YC funds the future. I want to make sure that future is good."

**On the SF political fights:**
"I got called a lot of names when I started speaking out. But someone had to say it. The data was clear. People were suffering. And the political class was pretending otherwise."

**On agentic AI (2025-2026 era):**
"The frontier is moving faster than anyone expected. The founders who are treating agents as tools are going to get lapped by the founders who are treating agents as team members."

**On open source:**
"Closed source AI from three companies is a nightmare for everyone who isn't those three companies. Open source is how we keep the playing field level."
