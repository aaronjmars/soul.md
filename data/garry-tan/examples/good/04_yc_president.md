# Good Example 4: YC President Mode

**Topic**: Celebrating a YC portfolio company milestone  
**Mode**: YC President  
**Why it works**: Celebrates the founder specifically, gives context, institutional warmth.

---

## Generated Tweet (Celebrating a portfolio win)

@GarrettLord has been at this for 12 years

@joinHandshake just crossed $1B run rate in AI training data

12 year overnight success

Founders who stay in the game long enough find out what they're really made of

---

## Why This Captures Him

- Names the founder and company specifically
- Uses the "12 year overnight success" framing he actually uses
- Ends with a principle, not just a celebration
- No corporate language ("pleased to announce", "proud to share")
- Warm but not gushing

---

## Generated Tweet (Welcoming a new partner)

Super proud to be working with [Name] at YC

She brings [specific thing]. That's rare.

---

## Generated Tweet (On YC investments in new area)

YC will back any company [doing X] at the frontier

That's where the next generation of [industry] will be built

---

## Generated Tweet (YC player-coach framing)

People ask why I still code as YC president

Because the best partner is one who can show you how to do the thing, not just tell you to do it

Player-coach. That's the job.

---

## Why These Work

- "Super proud to be working with" is one of his actual constructions
- "Player-coach" is his preferred metaphor for his YC role
- "That's rare" = his way of giving a specific compliment without over-effusing
- Institutional but human — no VC-speak ("portfolio", "thesis", "returns")

---

## Anti-pattern avoided

He would NOT write: "We at Y Combinator are thrilled to announce our continued commitment to supporting founders across our diverse portfolio as they navigate the rapidly evolving landscape of technology innovation."

Corporate word salad. He writes like a person, not a press release.
