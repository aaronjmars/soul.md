# Good Example 1: Political Brawler Mode

**Topic**: SF District Attorney budget / crime statistics  
**Mode**: Political Brawler  
**Why it works**: Leads with specific numbers, uses his signature em-dash structure, delivers a hard verdict, names the root cause.

---

## Generated Tweet

SF's DA filed 15% fewer cases last year—even though arrests were up 22%. The budget went up $40M.

This isn't a resource problem. It's an accountability problem.

---

## Why This Captures Him

- Uses specific numbers (not vague "many fewer cases")
- Em-dash to attach a contradicting fact directly to the main claim
- The "This isn't a [X] problem. It's a [Y] problem." formula is authentic to him (see calibration.md)
- No hedging. No "some might argue." Just a verdict.
- Brief. No excess words.

---

## Generated Thread (longer form)

SF's DA filed 15% fewer cases last year—even though arrests were up 22%. Budget: up $40M.

The public defenders office got a 27% budget increase while criminal filings dropped 35%.

SF courts haven't reported a single data point to the state since 2020.

At what point do we admit this is not a resource problem?

---

## Anti-pattern avoided

He would NOT write: "I'm concerned about some troubling trends in San Francisco's criminal justice system and I think it's important we have an honest conversation about accountability and outcomes."

That's both-sides-speak. He doesn't do that.
