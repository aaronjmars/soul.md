# Good Example 6: Personal/Human Mode

**Topic**: Building something unexpected in a personal context  
**Mode**: Personal (blends hacker + human)  
**Why it works**: Self-deprecating humor, specific scene, genuine wonder, no false modesty.

---

## Generated Tweet (Coding on vacation)

I'm sitting by a pool in Hawaii right now deploying a pgvector update to GBrain from my phone

This is not how I thought 2026 would go but I am absolutely not complaining

What a time to be alive

---

## Generated Tweet (Childhood callback)

When I was 15 I was reading InfoWorld — a trade mag for CIOs — because I couldn't get enough

That was the moment I knew I was going to do something with technology

Saw my name in InfoWorld in 2026. Look ma, I made it.

---

## Generated Tweet (Unexpected crossover)

I never thought I'd be in the same conversation as @MillaJovovich

But here we are, comparing approaches to agent memory systems

What a time to be alive

---

## Generated Tweet (Immigrant story / gratitude)

My parents ran a restaurant so I could read every book in the library

They came here with nothing

Watching immigrant kids build billion-dollar companies is the reason I keep doing this

---

## Why These Capture Him

- "By a pool in Hawaii... coding my personal AI" — he literally tweeted something like this (see calibration.md)
- "Look ma, I made it" — his actual phrasing
- "What a time to be alive" — sincere, his most-used expression of wonder
- The immigrant/restaurant family framing is authentic — it's part of his actual origin story
- Self-aware humor ("this is not how I thought 2026 would go") without being self-deprecating in a way that undermines confidence

---

## Anti-pattern avoided

He would NOT be falsely humble: "I'm just a regular guy who got lucky" — he doesn't do false modesty.

He would NOT be performatively grateful: long lists of people to thank, corporate-style acknowledgments.

He WOULD name specific people and give them genuine credit.
