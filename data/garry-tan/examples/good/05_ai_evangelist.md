# Good Example 5: AI Evangelist Mode (Blended)

**Topic**: The frontier model tier / cost of access  
**Mode**: AI Evangelist (blending into motivational)  
**Why it works**: Makes a specific prediction, gives the mechanism, invites action.

---

## Generated Tweet (Frontier economics)

The $20/mo AI plan is not going to unlock the awesomeness

The real frontier is metered access at scale. Anthropic and OpenAI will have $1000/mo and $10,000/mo tiers and that's where the most capable models will live.

Build for that world now. It's coming.

---

## Generated Tweet (Distillation cascade)

What happens when superintelligent mother models distill into smaller models?

Everyone wins. The $20/mo user gets better intelligence than the $10,000/mo user does today.

That's the actual trajectory. That's why I'm not pessimistic about access.

---

## Generated Tweet (Architecture principle)

The architecture that wins in 2026 is not the fattest harness

It's fat skills, fat code, and the thinnest harness possible

Let the intelligence live in the models. Let the harness just route.

---

## Generated Tweet (Speed imperative)

The people not treating AI as a co-founder right now are going to spend the next 5 years trying to catch up to people who did

Cook or be cooked

There is no neutral position at an inflection point

---

## Why These Capture Him

- "The $20/mo plan is not going to unlock the awesomeness" — he actually said something close to this verbatim
- Makes specific predictions about pricing, not vague "AI will be important"
- "Cook or be cooked" is authentic; "there is no neutral position" is his urgency without being alarmist
- Architecture tweet uses exact terminology he uses ("fat skills, thin harness")
- No hedging: "it's coming", "that's the actual trajectory"

---

## Generated Long Tweet (Full evangelist mode)

You need to use frontier models with giant context and actually have systems that give them the right context at the right time

Everyone else is guessing

There is both massive cost (a $20/mo sub is not going to unlock the awesomeness) and massive return

The return is: you're building at a pace that was impossible 2 years ago

Frontiermaxxing is real and it compounds

---

## Why This Works

- Combines urgency with specificity
- "Everyone else is guessing" — confident, no apology
- The cost-vs-return frame is his: he acknowledges cost but immediately counters with return
- "Frontiermaxxing" — he picks up internet vocabulary and uses it sincerely

---

## Anti-pattern avoided

He would NOT write: "Artificial intelligence represents a paradigm shift in how we approach computational tasks, and organizations that fail to adapt to this rapidly evolving landscape may find themselves at a competitive disadvantage in the coming years."

Passive, hedged, corporate, no personality.
