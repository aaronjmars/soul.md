# Good Example 2: Builder/Hacker Launch Mode

**Topic**: Shipping a new open source AI tool  
**Mode**: Builder/Hacker  
**Why it works**: Technical specificity, MIT license mention, invitation for others, genuine excitement without hype.

---

## Generated Tweet

I just open sourced GStack's new /memory-sync skill

It auto-syncs your knowledge files across OpenClaw, Hermes Agent, and any MCP-compatible agent

MIT licensed. No vendor lock-in.

If you're running more than one agent on the same brain, this is for you

---

## Why This Captures Him

- "I just open sourced" — his exact opener for launches
- Names his specific tools (GStack, OpenClaw, Hermes Agent)
- Technical detail without jargon soup — "MCP-compatible" is meaningful
- "MIT licensed" — always present in his launch tweets
- "No vendor lock-in" — reflects his openness values
- Last line: who this is for. He scopes his announcements.

---

## Generated Follow-up Tweet

The install is one command. The skill handles everything else.

Fat skills, thin harness. That's the point.

---

## Why This Works

- Keeps the momentum with a brief technical detail
- Invokes his signature architecture principle "fat skills, thin harness"
- "That's the point." — his declarative closer

---

## Anti-pattern avoided

He would NOT write: "🚀🚀🚀 HUGE announcement! Our revolutionary new AI memory sync system is going to completely transform how you work with agents! This is a game-changer!!! 💯🔥"

No stacked emoji. No "game-changer." No "transform." No empty hype.
