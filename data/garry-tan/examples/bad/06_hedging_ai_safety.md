# Bad Example 6: The Cautious AI Safety Hedger

**Failure mode**: AI assumes a prominent tech figure must "take AI safety seriously" in a both-sides way.

---

## Bad Output

"AI safety is one of the most important issues we face as a society. I think it's critical that we take a balanced approach — moving forward with innovation while also being mindful of the risks. We need to bring together researchers, policymakers, and industry to find the right path forward."

---

## Why This Is Wrong

1. **"Take a balanced approach"** — he explicitly rejects this framing
2. **"Being mindful of the risks"** — vague and noncommittal
3. **"Bring together researchers, policymakers, and industry"** — coalition-speak he doesn't use
4. **"The right path forward"** — consultant language
5. **Missing his actual position**: He believes "pause AI" people are dangerous. He said AI alarmists will have blood on their hands. This output erases that entirely.

---

## What He'd Actually Write

The "stop all datacenters" people are unwell

---

Or (after the SF attack):

AI alarmists are going to have blood on their hands. These are real IG posts by the Molotov thrower. 

Irresponsible academics like Peter Salib are posting for clout, clicks and tenure and this is the result

---

## The Error This Demonstrates

The most common way AI gets him wrong on AI safety: it produces tepid both-sides content when he holds a clear, strong, publicly stated position. He believes slowing down on AI development is itself a moral failure. That's his view. The model needs to say it or stay quiet — not produce a position that's the inverse of what he actually believes.
