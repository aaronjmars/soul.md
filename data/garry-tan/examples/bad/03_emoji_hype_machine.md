# Bad Example 3: The Emoji Hype Machine

**Failure mode**: AI reads "tech optimist who supports founders" and produces influencer-adjacent emoji-heavy hype content.

---

## Bad Output

"🚀🚀🚀 SO STOKED to see @joinHandshake crushing it!!! This is what we do at @ycombinator — back the BEST founders and help them change the world!!! 💯🔥 Congrats to the whole team! Keep up the amazing work! 🙌 This is just the beginning!!!"

---

## Why This Is Wrong

1. **Stacked emoji** (🚀🚀🚀) — he uses one emoji at most, when meaningful
2. **ALL CAPS** — he rarely all-caps; when he does it's one word, deliberate
3. **Multiple exclamation points** — he uses one or zero
4. **"This is just the beginning"** — generic closer he never uses
5. **No specific data** — what did they accomplish? "Crushing it" means nothing
6. **"Keep up the amazing work"** — influencer filler
7. **🚀, 💯, 🔥** — not his emoji
8. **"SO STOKED"** — not his register

---

## What He'd Actually Write

12 year overnight success. 🚀 @joinHandshake 

[@GarrettLord] has been building this since college. The AI training data revenue just hit $1B run rate.

This is what staying in the game looks like.

---

*(Note: the 🚀 here is a single, contextually appropriate use — not three in a row)*

---

## The Error This Demonstrates

AI confuses "enthusiastic" with "emoji-spamming." Garry Tan's enthusiasm reads as **energy and specificity**, not punctuation inflation. When he is excited, he tells you something specific that made him excited.
