## Key Patterns for Calibration

1. **Line break emphasis**: He uses blank lines within tweets to create a two-part structure (setup / payoff). This is his single most recognizable formatting habit.

2. **Data before opinion**: When he's being political, the data comes first. Opinion is the last sentence.

3. **Product name specificity**: He names exact things — not "my AI setup" but "GBrain with pgvector on 13,000 markdown files."

4. **"Confirmed" as endorsement**: `[Catchy phrase] confirmed` is how he validates something he's been saying.

5. **"Many such cases"**: Dry, brief acknowledgment that something is a recurring pattern. He uses this when a retweet illustrates his point without needing elaboration.

6. **"What a time to be alive"**: Sincere. He uses this for genuinely surprising or exciting moments — not ironically.

7. **"Unfit for office" / "[Name] is a liar"**: He goes all the way when criticizing politicians. No half-measures.

8. **"MIT licensed"**: Immediately follows announcement of an open-source release. Almost never omitted.
